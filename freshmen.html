Freshmen (English 9)

Disclaimer: The following is the curriculum that all students in English 9 follow including the skills that are meant to be acquired in the school year. This section presents how AI can be used as a tool to advance the curriculum and instances where students may be able to use software in their assignments. Please ensure that AI is being used as a tool in your academic work, not as software abused to do an entire assignment for you. Academic integrity and the respective use of AI is our main goal.

Recommended AIs

Keep in mind that these are only suggestions. Students are open to using all AI tools listed in the "AI Usage Guidelines" section under "Which AI Tools are Acceptable?" Teachers are also welcome to guide their students in a certain direction. All tools must be used with the permission and discretion of a student's teacher. Misuse of AI will result in consequences.

QuillBot

When students are conducting research, they can utilize the tool QuillBot. Students can use QuillBot's paraphrasing and summarization features to condense complex texts into digestible points.

ChatGPT

ChatGPT can help generate ideas or questions, suggest sources or databases, and propose essay outlines. Use with teacher discretion and never to complete whole assignments.

English 9 Curriculum

Curriculum Purpose
Curriculum Revision: Aimed to enhance educational outcomes per New Jersey state standards. Incorporation of New Texts: Includes contemporary literature and climate change education. Social Emotional Learning (SEL): Integrated throughout the curriculum for emotional and social development. Problem-Based Learning: Encourages critical thinking and practical knowledge application. Interdisciplinary Connections: Focus on applying skills across various subjects.

Units of Study

  • Unit 1: Perceptions & Misconceptions
  • Unit 2: Self & Journey
  • Unit 3: Power & Equity
  • Unit 4: Survival & Evolution

Timeframe for Each Unit

6-8 weeks

When can I Use AI?

Research: Use AI to gather information and explore perspectives.

Text Analysis: AI can help annotate and identify literary devices (teacher discretion).

Collaborative Work: AI can assist with dividing tasks and generating discussion points.
