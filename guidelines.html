AI Usage Guidelines

Acceptable AI usage, consequences, permitted tools, and boundaries for students.

Acceptable AI Usage / Disclaimer

The following is a list of situations where students would be permitted to use AI in their work. Please keep in mind that all permitted uses must align with the student's grade level and the required skills. Despite these approved AI software, ensure that AI is being used as a tool in your academic work, not as software abused to do an entire assignment for you. Academic integrity and the respective use of AI is our main goal.

When can I use AI?

  • Generating questions — AI software can be used to generate guiding questions that can be used to answer or outline your essay. Use with teacher discretion.
  • Proofreading — Use AI to proofread writing, check structure, and align to rubrics.
  • Research — Use AI to filter sources, summarize provided texts, and find keywords; always verify sources.

When is AI Unacceptable?

AI must not be used as a first- or last-resort to complete assignments or to replace the student's own analysis and arguments. Specific instances where AI is strictly prohibited include using AI to write arguments and claims that should be generated by a student.

Consequences & Impact

Transcript Impact

If a student is caught misusing AI, the record may appear on their final high school transcript, which can negatively affect college applications, honors societies, and scholarships.

Classroom relationship impact

Using AI to produce assignment submissions hinders a teacher's ability to accurately assess a student's skills and can damage trust and classroom expectations.

Which AI Tools are Acceptable?

Gemini
Gemini is Google's multimodal AI-powered assistant meaning it can respond to various content types such as text, video, audio, programming code, and images. Despite being a reliable source in some situations, AI is subject to glitches and may not always perform reliably.

Claude
Claude excels at analyzing and responding with natural language processing software and is also a multimodal AI software. Claude accepts text, audio, and visual inputs and can answer questions, summarize documents, and generate long-form text, diagrams, animations, program code, etc. 

ChatGPT
ChatGPT is a generative AI tool that uses machine learning to produce human-like responses. It can generate responses to questions or requests for clarification while also being able to create written content like articles, essays, social media posts, emails, codes, etc. The software uses reinforcement learning to improve its responses based on human feedback and reward models. 

Microsoft Co-Pilot
Microsoft Copilot is an AI-powered digital assistant software that can be accessible through Windows, Microsoft 365, Word, PowerPoint, Excel, OneNote, and Outlook. Copilot makes it clear how the system makes decisions by noting limitations, linking to sources, and prompting users to review the content that is produced. The system also uses content in Microsoft Graph, like emails, chats, and documents that users have permission to access. 

Notebook LM
Notebook LM is an AI-powered research tool produced by Google that allows users to upload documents and present the software with any questions they may have about the text. Notebook LM will then provide detailed answers and summaries based on only the information presented in the text.   

Perplexity AI
Perplexity AI is a search engine and a conversational AI tool that can be used to provide answers to questions that are asked by the software. Perplexity AI is able to provide information and citations in real time, ensuring the incorporation of various data sources. Despite this, sources may not always be scholarly and should always be reviewed. The software can also be useful in data management, project management, and providing solutions to resolve conflicts. 

Sourcely 
Sourcely is an AI-powered academic search assistant that aids in streamlining your research through exposure to credible sources, summaries, and citations. The essential goal is to serve the purpose of saving time for students when writing large pieces. Start by pasting your essay into the software and you will receive a credibly sourced piece after the AI has worked through it. Sourcely can also give summaries of the pieces that are pasted into its software.  

QuillBot
QuillBot offers various writing tools powered by its AI software including features that help fine-tune writing. For example, the software offers grammar checking, a paraphraser, a plagiarism checker, a citation generator, an AI content detector, a translator, a citation helper, etc.

ZeroGPT
ZeroGPT is an AI text detector that analyzes text to ensure that it is human-written, flagging any AI-generated content. The software will provide a percentage of AI-generated content, providing input like "Your text is human-written" or "Your text is most likely AI/GPT generated." The software can detect multiple languages and is also available on WhatsApp and Telegram where it can also summarize, paraphrase, translate, and check for grammar errors.

Note: Tools listed are suggestions; teachers have discretion and all use must follow district policy.
