Sophomores (English 10)

Disclaimer: The following is the curriculum that all students in English 10 follow including the skills that are meant to be acquired in the school year. This section presents how AI can be used as a tool to advance the curriculum and instances where students may be able to use software in their assignments. Please ensure that AI is being used as a tool in your academic work, not as software abused to do an entire assignment for you. Academic integrity and the respective use of AI is our main goal.

Recommended AIs

Keep in mind that these are only suggestions. Students are open to using all AI tools listed in the "AI Usage Guidelines" section under "Which AI Tools are Acceptable?" Teachers are also welcome to guide their students in a certain direction. All tools must be used with the permission and discretion of a student's teacher. Misuse of AI will result in consequences.

QuillBot

When students are conducting research, they can utilize the tool QuillBot. The tool is mentioned and detailed in the "AI Usage Guidelines" section under "Which AI Tools Are Acceptable?" Students can use QuillBot's paraphrasing and summarization features to condense complex texts into digestible points.

ChatGPT

ChatGPT can help generate ideas, outlines, and suggest sources. Use with teacher discretion and never to complete entire assignments.

English 10 Curriculum

Unit 1: The American Experience (8-10 Weeks) — Through studying texts that address various identity factors, students begin to understand how society impacts an individual and the consequences that arise from the choice of action or inaction.

Key Concepts & Units

  • Unit 1: The American Experience — evolving concept of American identity; literature's impact on societal and individual perspectives; analysis of race, gender, and identity.
  • Unit 2: The American Understanding of Equality — examining diverse perspectives through literature and how they impact social movements.
  • Unit 3: The American Quest for Equality — explores movements, figures, and struggles shaping justice and fairness.
  • Unit 4: The American Dream — examines how the American Dream has changed over time and its impact on different groups.

When can I Use AI?

Research: The AI resources listed in the section above can be used to help students gather information, explore different perspectives, and access a range of sources to support their analysis.

Writing Assistance: AI can help organize thoughts, generate outlines, suggest ideas, and help refine arguments for writing tasks. Use as a tool, not a replacement.

Text Analysis & Collaborative Work: AI can aid annotation support, identify devices, and assist with group tasking and discussion prompts.
